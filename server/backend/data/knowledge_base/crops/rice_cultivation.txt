Rice Cultivation in Kerala

Rice (Oryza sativa) is the staple food crop of Kerala. The state has three main growing seasons:

1. Virippu (May-June to September-October): Main monsoon season
2. Mundakan (December-January to March-April): Post-monsoon season  
3. Puncha (April-May to July-August): Summer season

Soil Requirements:
- Well-drained, fertile alluvial soil
- pH range: 5.5 to 7.0
- Good water retention capacity

Seed Treatment:
- Treat seeds with Trichoderma viride @ 4g/kg of seeds
- Soak seeds for 24 hours before sowing

Nursery Management:
- Prepare nursery beds 1.2m wide and 10m long
- Apply organic manure @ 2 kg/m²
- Sow pre-treated seeds @ 30-40g/m²

Transplanting:
- Transplant 15-21 day old seedlings
- Spacing: 20cm x 15cm for high-yielding varieties
- Use 2-3 seedlings per hill

Water Management:
- Maintain 2-5cm water level during active tillering
- Drain field 7-10 days before harvest

Fertilizer Application:
- Urea: 90 kg/ha (split into 3 doses)
- Single Super Phosphate: 125 kg/ha (basal dose)
- Muriate of Potash: 50 kg/ha (split into 2 doses)

Major Diseases:
- Blast: Caused by Pyricularia oryzae
- Bacterial Leaf Blight: Caused by Xanthomonas oryzae
- Brown Spot: Caused by Helminthosporium oryzae

Major Pests:
- Yellow stem borer: Scirpophaga incertulas
- Brown plant hopper: Nilaparvata lugens  
- Leaf folder: Cnaphalocrocis medinalis
